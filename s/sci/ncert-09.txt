 (a) Quick lime *Calcium, oxygen*

 (a) Ethyne, C2 H2 *26 g*

*8-force-and-laws-of-motion*
Speed
  - metre per second (SI Unit)
  - m s^-1

Average speed = Total distance travelled / Total time taken

Velocity
  - both speed and direction

Average velocity = initial velocity + final velocity / 2

Acceleration = change in velocity / time taken
 a = v - u /t 
 m/s^2 (SI Unit of acceleration)


Eg 8.1 An object travels 16 m in 4 s and then another 16 m in 2 s. What is the average speed of the object?
   => Total distance travelled by the object =
   => 16 m + 16 m = 32 m
   => Total time taken = 4 s + 2 s = 6 s
   => Average speed = Total distance travelled / Total time taken
   => 32m / 6s
   => 5.33 m s^-1

Eg 8.2 The odometer of a car reads 2000 km at the start of a trip and  2400 km at the end of the trip. If the trip took 8 h, calculate the average speed of the car in km h–1 and m s–1.
  => s = 2400 km – 2000 km = 400 km
  => Time elapsed, t = 8 h
  => Average speed of car,
  => V(av) =  s/t
  => 400 km / 8 h
  => 50 km/h
  => 50km/h x 1000 m / 1 km x 1 h / 3600 s
  => 13.9 m s^-1
