*1-matter-in-our-surroundings*

Kinetic Energy
  - Energy an object has because of its motion

Melting point of ice
  -> 273.15K

Boiling point of water
  -> 373K

*Sublimition* 
  - Solid to gas

*Deposition* 
  - Gas to solid

Atmosphere (atm) 
  - Unit of measuring pressure 

Acetone
  - In nail polish

Bose Einstein Condensate (BEC)
  - 5th state of matter
  - Nobel pr. 2001
  - Cooling a gas of extremely low density
  - Eric A. Cornell; Wolfgang Ketterle; Carl E. Wieman

*Latent heat* of vaporisation 
  - Heat energy required to change 1 kg of liquid to gas at atmospheric pressure at its boiling point

*Quantity-Unit-Symbol*
Temperature
  - Kelvin (K)
Weight
  - Newton (N)
Volume
  - Cubic metre (m^3)
Density
  - kilogram per cubic metre (kg m^3)

TAG:3-exercises
*Ch-3-exercises*
Q1. A 0.24 g sample of compound of oxygen and boron was found
by analysis to contain 0.096 g of boron and 0.144 g of oxygen.
Calculate the percentage composition of the compound by
weight. (boton: 40%, oxygen: 60%)

Q2. When 3.0 g of carbon is burnt in 8.00 g oxygen, 11.00 g of 
carbon dioxide is produced. What mass of carbon dioxide will be formed when 3.00 g  of carbon is burnt in 50.00 g of
oxygen? Which law of chemical combination will govern your
answer? (50 - 8 = 42g of O2)

Q3. What are polyatomic ions?  Give examples.

Q4. Write the chemical formulae of the following.
 (a) Magnesium chloride *MgCl2*
 (b) Calcium oxide *CaO*
 (c) Copper nitrate *Cu (NO3)2*
 (d) Aluminium chloride  *AlCl3*
 (e) Calcium carbonate  *CaCO3*

Q5. Give the names of the elements present in the following
 compounds.
 (a) Quick lime *Calcium, oxygen*
 (b) Hydrogen bromide *Hydrogen, bromine*
 (c) Baking powder *Sodium, hydrogen, carbon and oxygen*
 (d) Potassium sulphate *Potassium, sulphur and oxygen*

Q6. Calculate the molar mass of the following substances.
 (a) Ethyne, C2 H2 *26 g*
 (b) Sulphur molecule, S8 *256 g*
 (c) Phosphorus molecule, P4 (Atomic mass of phosphorus = 31) *124 g*
 (d) Hydrochloric acid, HCl *36.5 g*
 (e) Nitric acid, HNO3 *63 g*

Q7. What is the mass of—
 (a) 1 mole of nitrogen atoms? *14 g*
 (b) 4 moles of aluminium atoms (Atomic mass of aluminium= 27)? *108 g*
 (c) 10 moles of sodium sulphite (Na2SO3)? *1260 g*

Q8. Convert into mole.
 (a) 12 g of oxygen gas *0.375 mole*
 (b) 20 g of water *1.11 mole*
 (c) 22 g of carbon dioxide. *0.5 mole* 

Q9. What is the mass of:
 (a) 0.2 mole of oxygen atoms? *3.2 g*
 (b) 0.5 mole of water molecules? *9.0 g*

Q10. Calculate the number of molecules of sulphur (S8) present in 16 g of solid sulphur. *3.76 × 10^22 molecules*

Q11. Calculate the number of aluminium ions present in 0.051 g
 of aluminium oxide.
 (Hint: The mass of an ion is the same as that of an atom of
 the same element. Atomic mass of Al = 27 u)  *6.022 × 1020 ions*

*Polyatomic ions*
  - Ions which contain more than one atom and behave as a single unit. Eg. OH^-, SO4^2-, CO3^2-

*4-structure-of-the-atom*

*JJ Thomson* (1856-1940)
  - Nobel prize (1906)
    - discover of electrons

*E. Rutherford* (1871-1937)
  - "father of nuclear physics"
  - radioactivity
  - discovery of the nucleus of atom
  - gold foil experiment
  - Nobel prize: 1908

*Antonie Laurent Lavoiser*
  - "father of Chemistry"

*Neils Bohr* (1885-1962)
  - Nobel prize: 1916
    - Structure of Atom
  - Books:
    - The Theory of Spectra and Atomic Constitution
    - Atomic Theory
    - the Description of Nature

*J chadwick*
  - discovered neuton (1932)
