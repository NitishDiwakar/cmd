Global Multidimensional Poverty Index : 2024

- Issued by : UNDP (Released on October 17, 2024)
- 1.1 billion people around the world live in acute poverty.
- More than half of the poor population (584 million) are children under the age of 18.
- Many poor people are deprived of basic resources.
- Such as : 828 million (adequate sanitation), 886 million (housing) and 998 million (cooking fuel).
- India has the highest number of poor people (23.4 Crore) in the world

2nd National Multidimensional Poverty Index : 2023

- Issued by : NITI Aayog (Released on July, 2023)
- The poorest state : Bihar (33.76%) > Jharkhand > UP
- The least poor state : Kerala
- 13.5 crore people came out of poverty between 2015-16 and 2019-21.
- In rural areas : decline (32.59% to 19.28%)
- In urban areas : decline (8.65% to 5.27%)
- Intensity of poverty has reduced from 47% to 44% between 2015-16 and 2019-21.
- Substantial decline in total poverty headcount ration from 24.85% in 2015-16 to 14.96% in 2019-21.
