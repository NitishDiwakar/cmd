============================
Title : Geography of Bihar
Tags  : #
Descr :
============================

Location
- Eastern India
- Between *24°20′10″N to 27°31′15″N* latitude
- Between *83°19′50″E to 88°17′40″E* longitude

Area
- Total area *94,163* sq km
- *13th largest* state by area in India

Boundaries
- North Nepal
- East West Bengal
- West Uttar Pradesh
- South Jharkhand

Physiographic Divisions
- Northern Bihar Plain
- Southern Bihar Plain

Northern Bihar Plain
- Lies north of river Ganga
- Part of Indo Gangetic Plain
- Formed by Himalayan rivers
- Prone to flooding

Southern Bihar Plain
- Lies south of river Ganga
- Slightly elevated compared to northern plain
- Includes parts of Chota Nagpur Plateau fringe

Major Rivers
- Ganga
- Kosi
- Gandak
- Burhi Gandak
- Bagmati
- Kamla
- Mahananda
- Son

River System
- Most rivers flow from north to south
- Originate in Nepal Himalayas
- Ganga flows west to east across state

Soil Types
- Alluvial soil dominant
- New alluvium in north
- Old alluvium in south
- Fertile agricultural soil

Climate
- Humid subtropical climate
- Hot summer March to June
- Monsoon June to September
- Cool winter November to February

Rainfall
- Average annual rainfall about *1,200 mm*
- Higher rainfall in north
- Decreases towards southwest

Temperature
- Summer temperature up to 40°C
- Winter temperature may fall below 5°C

Natural Regions
- Terai region along Nepal border
- Gangetic plain
- Plateau fringe in south

Forest Cover
- Forest area mainly in southern districts
- Also in northwestern districts

Seismic Zone
- Falls in seismic zones IV and V
- High earthquake vulnerability
