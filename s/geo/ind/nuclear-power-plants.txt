Tamil Nadu
    - Kudankulam Nuclear Power Plant
        Capacity: 2,000 MW
        Note: India’s largest nuclear plant; built with Russian collaboration.
    - Madras Atomic Power Station (Kalpakkam)
        Capacity: 440 MW
        Note: India's first fully indigenous nuclear power station.

Gujarat
    - Kakrapar Atomic Power Station
        Capacity: 1,840 MW
        Note: Features the newest 700 MW indigenously designed reactors.

Rajasthan
    - Rajasthan Atomic Power Station (Rawatbhata)
        Capacity: 1,780 MW
        Note: Uses pressurized heavy water reactors (PHWR).

Maharashtra
    - Tarapur Atomic Power Station
        Capacity: 1,400 MW
        Note: India’s first nuclear power plant (established 1969).

Karnataka
    - Kaiga Generating Station
        Capacity: 880 MW
        Note: Located near the Kali River; serves the southern grid.

Uttar Pradesh
    - Narora Atomic Power Station
        Capacity: 440 MW
        Note: Located in a high seismic zone in Northern India.

Haryana (Upcoming)
    - Gorakhpur Haryana Anu Vidyut Pariyojana
        Status: Under Construction
        Note: Will be the first nuclear plant in North India outside UP/Rajasthan.
