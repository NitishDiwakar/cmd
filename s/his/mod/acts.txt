============================
Title : Acts
Tags  : #
Descr :
============================


Regulating Act of 1773
    - 1st step by British to control EIC
    - Recognised political & administrative functions of Company
    - Laid foundation of central administration in India
    - Ultimate sovereign power to British Crown
    - Court of Directors (60 members)
        * To submit civil, military & revenue communications
    - Supremacy of Fort William (Bengal) over Madras & Bombay
    - Governor-General (Warren Hastings) + 4 councillors
    - Company servants forbidden from private trade
    - Supreme Court established at Calcutta (1774)
        * 1 Chief Justice + 3 Judges


Amending (Judicature) Act, 1781
    - Extended SC jurisdiction to all inhabitants of Calcutta
    - Excluded GG & council for official acts


Pitt's India Act, 1784
    - Separated political & commercial functions
    - Board of Control created
        * 6 members
            - 2 from British Cabinet
            - 4 from Privy Council
    - GG Council reduced from 4 to 3
    - GG given veto power


Charter Act of 1793
    - Company trade monopoly extended for 20 years
    - Salaries of Board & staff charged to Company
    - GG veto power affirmed
    - Senior officials forbidden to leave India without permission
    - Royal approval required for:
        * Governor-General
        * Governor
        * Commander-in-Chief


Charter Act of 1813
    - EIC monopoly ended (except Tea & China trade)
    - Crown sovereignty asserted
    - 1 lakh rupees allotted for education
    - Christian missionaries permitted


Charter Act of 1833
    - Company commercial activities ended
    - Governor-General of Bengal became GG of India
    - Centralisation of legislative power


Charter Act of 1853
    - Separate legislative & executive functions of GG Council
    - Open competition system introduced (later implemented)
    - No fixed period for Company rule extension


Vernacular Press Act, 1878
    - Restrictions on non-English newspapers
    - Viceroy: Lord Lytton (1876–80)


Ilbert Bill, 1883
    - Indian judges allowed to try Europeans
    - Viceroy: Lord Ripon (1880–84)
