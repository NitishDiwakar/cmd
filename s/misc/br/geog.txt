*Soil*

*Soils of North Bihar Plain*

1. Piedmont Swamp Soil
    - North Western part of West Champaran
    - Bhabar plains
    - Rocks and pebble are predominant
    - Colour: brownish
    - Nature: acidic

2. Terai Soil
    - South of Someshwar range
    - Highest concentration - Araria
    - Crops: rice

3. Alluvial Soil
  
  3a. Khadar Soil (newer)
  3b. Bhangar Soil (older)


*Soils of North Bihar Plain*

1. Old Alluvial Soil
    - South Bihar, where flood water never reach
    - Rohtas, Gaya, Aurangabad, Patna, Jehanabad, Munger, Bhagalpur
    - heavy *Clay Soil*, - *Kewal soil*
    - Colour: brown to yellow
    - Nature: balanced with alkalinity and acidic

2. Tal Soil
    - Buxar to Banka
    - remains water logged for months due to rain water
    - Colour: light grey to dark grey
    - Unsuitable for kharif growth
    - Crops: Pulses, oilseed, wheat

3. *Balthar Soil*
    - AKA Red and Yellow soil
    - between Chhotanagpur plateau and South Ganga plain
    - Narrow belt extending from *Kaimur to Rajmahal hills*
    - Colour: red (due to presence of iron)
    - less fertile
    - suitable for: Maize, jowar-bajra, gram

4. Red Sandy Soil
    - Kaimur and Rohtas
    - High percentage of sand
