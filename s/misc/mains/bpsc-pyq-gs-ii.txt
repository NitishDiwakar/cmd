*Section - I*

1. Write short answer of the following questions:

  a. Briefly describe the main features and significance of *42nd Constitutional Amendment* Act of 1976. [8 marks]

  b. There has been conflicting debate about Supremacy between Parliament and Judiciary in the light of debate, explain your views. [8 marks]

  c. Explain the concept of '*Writ Jurisdiction*' and name any three of the five writs that can be issued by the Supreme Court or High Courts to enforce Fundamental Rights. [8 marks]

  d. Define *Judical Review* in the context of Indian Constitution. What is its basic purpose? [7 marks]

  e. What is the Primary role of the *Finance Commission* of India? Which article provides for its establishment? [7 marks]


2a. Is there any meeting points between *Cultural Nationalism* and *Constitutional Nationalism*. Explain some basic features of cultural understanding under the Indian Constitution. [38]

OR

2b. "*Constitutional Morality* is not a natural sentiment but has to be cultivated". In light of this statement, critically analyse the Concept of 'Constitutional Morality' as propounded by the Supre Court of India. [38]


3a. Parliament's power to amend the Constitution is limited power and it cannot be enlarged into absolute power. Can Parliament under *Article 368* can destroy the Basic Structure of the Constitution when it enjoys absolute majority in Parliament? [38]

OR

3b. Cricically analyze the impact of India's multi-party system on the stability and functions of its *federal structure*. To what extent have coalition governments strengthened or weakened the cooperative and competitive dimensions of *Indian federalism*? Explain. [38 marks]


*Section - II*

4. Write short answer of the following question:

  a. Discuss the major challenges facing India's *food processing industry*. What initiatives have been taken by government for the improvement? [8 marks]

  b. *Jute* is in great demand because of cheapness, softness, strength, length and uniformity of its fibre. Explain its favourable conditions suitable for the Jute cultivation in West Bengal and bihar. [8]

  c. Describe the role of natural resource management, inclusive development and *e-technology* for achieving the goal of a *Atmanirbhar Bharat* and *Viksit Bharat*. [8]

  d. Give an account of the environment and social benefits of *geothermal energy* in India with suitable examples. [7]

  e. Describe the initiatives taken by government to enhance inclusive and sustainable *development of South Bihar* in last 10 years. [7]


5a. Discuss the *role of railway* in the *development of country*. Critically analyze the geographical, economic and political challenges faced by Indian Railways in enhancing its logistics efficiency in north-eastern partof country?

OR

5b. What are the components of *food security*? How for *hydroponic agriculture* will be helpful to fulfil the future domestic demands in country? Justify it with suitable examples. [38]


6a. Discuss the *major ports* on *eastern coast* of India. Critically examine the role of Syama Prasad Mookerjee Port, Kolkata (SMPK) in the development of North East India and Bangladesh, giving suitable examples. [38]


6b. Describe the concept of *fourth industrial revolution* (Industry 4.0). Critically analyse the biggest benefits and risks of adopting industry 4.0 technologies in India. [38]


*Section - III*

7. Write short answer of the following questions:

  a. What role has the philosophy of "*Jai Anusandhan, Jai Anuprayog*" played in the process of technological Indigenization in India? Critically analyze India's current state of technological self reliance in the field of *artificial Intelligence and Robotics*. [6]

  b. To what extent has expansion of *STEM education in Bihar* been successful in bridgin regional educational disparities? Discuss the hurdles of '*Digital Divide*' and '*Teacher capacity*', in the context of providing quality education. [6]

  c. Explain how the integration of *nano technology* with environmental monitoring systems can meaningfully address pollution and degradation in *India's Industrial belts*. Support your answer with examples of emerging innovations. [6]

  d. How has the use of *remote sensing satellites* in *disaster management* redefined the principle of "*Disaster Risk Reduction*"? Explain the role of India's *NETRA* project with suitable examples. [6]

  e. The high end *indigenization of space*, IT and defence is believed to be the base of India's long term strategic autonomy. Examine the key institutional, financial and technological barriers that still inhibit complete indigenization, despite considerable national progress. [6]

  f. How do *social networking sites* act as a 'force multipliers' in the challenges to *internal security* posed through communication networks? Analyze the judicial and technical implications of this internal security versus civil liberty dilemma. [6]


8a. In the perspective of the basics of *cyber security*, the need for digital sovereignty and the effectiveness of the *Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA)*, suggest components for comprehensive internal security strategy. [36]

OR

Is this transition merely about technological development, or has it qualitatively transformed India's capabilities for *national security*, *disaster response* and *border management*? In the context of *drone* warm technology, the security of satellite based communication and the strategic significance of *GSLV* program, propose the essential pillars of a comprehensive 'Security-Development-Innovation' strategy for India's space and allied technologies. [36]
